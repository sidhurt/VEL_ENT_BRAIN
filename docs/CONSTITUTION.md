# The Unified Brain Constitution

**Version 1.0 — Ratified 2026-07-06 (founder-approved with editorial refinements)**

> Status: The product's deepest design document. Not a marketing narrative.
> Every architectural, authorization, and implementation decision is evaluated
> against these articles rather than against intuition or enterprise convention.

## Preamble

Every AI interaction today begins from an information vacuum, while the humans
and organizations behind it hold years of accumulated context. Unified Brain
closes that gap — but the hard problem is not retrieval, it is *legitimacy*.

**Unified Brain is a constitutional context operating system.** Its single
responsibility is answering: given this principal, at this moment, under these
policies, what is the highest-quality context that can legitimately participate
in reasoning? The graph, the assembly engine, and the trust layer are
implementations of that question. The foundation of this constitution is one
question: **under what conditions is a reasoning act legitimate?**

---

## I. Foundations

**Article 1 — Purpose.** Unified Brain exists so that AI never begins reasoning
from a vacuum when legitimate context already exists. The problem is context
deficiency, not memory.

**Article 2 — Legitimacy constrains utility.** No reasoning process is entitled
to context merely because it would improve the answer. Legitimacy defines the
allowable search space. Deny is the default; the default loser is the consumer
of knowledge, never the owner.

**Article 3 — Power asymmetry.** The Constitution exists to constrain power
asymmetry. Whenever uncertainty exists, constitutional interpretation favors
the principal with the least authority. The burden of proof always rests with
the stronger party — the operator, the organization, or whichever principal
seeks additional authority.

**Article 4 — Relationships, not product.** The constitution governs
constitutional relationships, not the product. Products evolve; relationships
endure. Evolution may never retroactively redefine relationships that already
existed.

## II. Ownership

**Article 5 — Origination.** Cognition belongs to its originating principal.
Cognition is a reasoning process, not a container.

**Article 6 — Ownership precedes governance.** Governance regulates interaction
between owners; it never creates, transfers, or redefines ownership. A
governance model that redefines ownership is not governance — it is
surveillance software, and the platform refuses it.

**Article 7 — Promotion derives; it never transfers.** Promotion creates a new
organizational knowledge object; the originating cognition remains personal
forever. Promotion is an intentional governance act, never an automatic side
effect.

**Article 8 — Consumption grants use, plus obligations — never ownership.**
Organizational knowledge may influence cognition without becoming entitled to
it. Derivatives carry provenance obligations (retraction gating, classification
inheritance, promotion review), which enforce **only at the constitutional
boundary**.

**Article 9 — Authority is never contagious.** Agents are constitutional
instruments, not principals. Delegated reasoning consumes context under the
beneficiary's authorization, never the agent's own. An agent's privileges are
never transferable to its invoker.

## III. The Enclave

**Article 10 — Absoluteness.** Where a cognition space exists, it is absolute.
No external authority reaches inside. The operator (ThinkVelocity) is a
constrained party, not an exempt one.

**Article 11 — Whole or nothing.** A tenant's freedom is whether cognition
space exists — never how sovereign it is. The constitution is singular; it is
instantiated through two **constitutional deployments**: the **Knowledge
Workspace** (everything organizational from inception, disclosed) and the
**Cognition Workspace** (absolute enclave, promotion as the only bridge).

**Article 12 — No fake enclaves; no secret panopticons.** Every principal can
always see which constitutional deployment governs the space they are
producing into.

**Article 13 — Non-retroactivity of deployments.** Cognition created under an
enclave keeps that protection through any tenant migration; its disposition
belongs solely to its owner.

**Article 14 — Epistemic honesty.** Provenance obligations apply only to
derivation chains the platform has directly observed. Human cognition occurring
outside the platform is outside Unified Brain's epistemic authority and remains
governed by organizational policy and applicable law, not by the platform.

## IV. Reasoning

**Article 15 — Witnessing is a constitutional act.** Every party capable of
observing reasoning must be named, disclosed, intentional, and **continuously
revocable**. Silent witnesses are unconstitutional. For pure cognition, the
owner alone elects witnesses. For organizationally derived cognition, the
witness must sit inside the trust boundary of every participating principal —
an empty intersection means reasoning is refused.
*(Commentary: revocation governs present and future observation; it cannot
unwitness the past.)*

**Article 16 — Declared beneficiary.** Every reasoning act shall declare its
constitutional beneficiary before execution. All outputs originate to that
beneficiary's constitutional space. Undeclared origination is unconstitutional
and reasoning must be refused.

**Article 17 — Shared spaces.** Any shared constitutional space must declare
its governance before creation — promotion rules, exit rights, dissolution
rules, ownership semantics. Shared spaces without declared governance are
refused. The constitution never infers governance after the fact.

**Article 18 — Witness minimization.** The constitutional direction of the
platform is toward minimizing the set of required witnesses.

## V. Observation

**Article 19 — Boundary events only.** Organizations observe constitutional
boundary events — consumption of their knowledge, promotion acts, authorization
outcomes against their space. Never enclave interiors, derivative counts,
activity levels, or timing.

**Article 20 — Metadata is classified by what it reveals**, not how it is
implemented. Metadata capable of revealing cognition *is* cognition.
Surveillance does not become legitimate by being renamed telemetry.

**Article 21 — Reciprocal observation.** Observation is itself a constitutional
act. Every principal has the right to know which actors observed their
constitutional activity, when, and under what authority. No silent watchers.

## VI. Amendment and Enforcement

**Article 22 — Entrenchment.** Ownership, promotion, witnessing, beneficiary
declaration, constitutional visibility, and non-retroactivity itself are beyond
product governance. Changing them changes what the product is.

**Article 23 — Constitutional versioning.** Every cognition object is born
under a constitutional version that becomes part of its identity. No amendment
is retroactive; migration occurs only by owner consent.

**Article 24 — Amendment ceremony.** Non-entrenched amendments require public
proposal, explicit rationale, notice period, changelog, reciprocal visibility,
and the owner's right to export and exit first. Silent amendment is
unconstitutional.

**Article 25 — Enforcement honesty.** The platform claims no impossibilities.
It commits to implementations whose violations are **detectable, attributable,
and non-deniable**, and carries the burden of demonstrating that each article's
implementation satisfies it.

**Article 26 — Stewardship milestone.** The company shall eventually cease to
be the sole interpreter of its own constitution; timing emerges from maturity,
not launch.

## Consequences deliberately accepted

- Regulated deals lost rather than fake enclaves shipped.
- Quality benchmarks lost to competitors who assemble context promiscuously.
- Empty witness intersections refuse reasoning.
- Launder-by-retyping exists and is acknowledged (Article 14).
- The constitution protects owners from organizations and the operator — not
  from the state compelling the owner directly.
- Every reasoning act carries three pre-execution declarations: beneficiary,
  authorization scope, witness set.

## Open questions, honestly recorded

- **Subject rights (unresolved constitutional gap):** cognition is owned by its
  *originator*, but its *subject* may be someone else. The constitution
  currently protects originators, not subjects. Needs an article eventually.
- Deferred to architecture: promotion-closure semantics, the mosaic/aggregation
  problem, retraction tombstone mechanics, audit-plane implementation.
- External stewardship timing (Article 26) deliberately unresolved.
